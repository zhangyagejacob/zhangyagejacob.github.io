Research

Myosin1e's contributes to regulation of invadosome dynamics"

Podosomes and invadopodia are actin-based dynamic protrusions which located on the periphery of the plasma membrane, and are associated with invasion and degradation of the extracellular matrix (ECM) in cancer invasiveness and metastasis. Myosin 1e(Myo1e) which expressed at the podosomes and invadopodia cores, is an actin-based motor protein that has been implicated in cell adhesion and migration. In this study, we did a rounded analysis of dynamical association of Myo1e and other relevant components.

Pinned deformation of particle-stabilized interface

The critical pressure that a liquid marble can withstand has been studied, which we now knew is related to the type and size of the stabilizing particles. However, how surface particles are distributed during the collision is unknown. We devised a new approach to detect surface particles' density distribution while the liquid marble is under mechanical collision. We also found that under squeezing, the particles density of the contact plane will not be affected by the successive mechanical compression. By testifying this property, our results demonstrate the position where rupture foremost occurs can be forecasted.

Digital platform for actuating liquid marbles

We show that liquid marbles can be actuated to move by an applied DC electric field. This electric field is achieved by charging a pair of electrodes from an open-plate printed circuit board (PCB) covered with a dielectric layer. We find that the liquid marble rolls freely and fast while retaining its non-sticky property. We anticipate our approach of moving liquid marbles to be a basis for more sophisticated manipulation of liquid marbles. We will demonstrate the potentials of our finding by its applications in biomedical research, which would benefit from avoiding cross-contamination or biofouling.
